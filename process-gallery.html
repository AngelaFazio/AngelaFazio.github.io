Process

Approaching Design and Development

I begin the process of design by obtaining the requirements and concentrating on the problem. I attempt to discover potential solutions, goals, and possibilities. From there, I may reframe the problem and try to understand more.

Depending on the project, I may dedicate some time finding inspiration or exploring different methods. Sometimes I will experiment with different technologies or artistic techniques. With certain projects, I may conduct or obtain research.

When I feel confident about my solutions, I collect all of my research, requirements, and solutions to development a plan. I will know which technologies, frameworks, techniques, and langauges I will be using. I will also have a timeline for deliverables.

This is where my skills begin to crossover. I can take ownership from this point forward or hand the prototypes over to the engineers. I speak both design and codez.

If I am working as the designer, I can begin creating wireframes, mockups, design assests, design style tiles, or living style guides.
...

If I am coding, I begin working on the prototype. I can hand code the prototypes, design static assests in Photoshop or dynamically with other software such as Axure, Flash, or After Effects.

At this point I can begin to work with back end engineers to start the process of building the product.
