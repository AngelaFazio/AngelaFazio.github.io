I'm Angela, a designer and developer.

How did I get here?

I began this adventure as a graphic designer. I worked hard and became very knowledgeable of print design and production. Eventually I started to feel the need to learn more and expand my skill set. That's what led me into development, I decided to give it a shot.

Eventually my curiosity led me to Code Fellows where took a UX Foundations course. This began to shine the light on the dark corners of the development world and it opened up a limitless world of possibility.

After the first course, I decided to take a Front-End UX Accelerator course at Code Fellows. The class was 8 weeks of fast and intense instruction. Not without a certain amount of stress, this was an amazing experience and I began to thrive in the fast paced environment. We had strict requirements to complete each project with a team or partner and we were encouraged to change partners/teams with each assignment. This was immensely valuable.

My development skills

As of right now, I am knowledgable of current trends and best practices in web development. One of the greatest lessons I have recently learned is how dynamic development can be, and as a front end engineer, I can never stop learning new things.

My workflow and the tools I use vary depending on the project. In general, I am a fan of agile methodologies and focusing on the minimum viable product. I can start a project and code it using scalability and relevant naming conventions with a focus on keeping it dry. I try to steer away from bloated and growth-limiting libraries, but I know when to use to right tools to get the job done! Even though I am a newbie, I have already worked with Html5, CSS, Sass, Jade, Jquery, JavaScript, Node.js, and Angular.
